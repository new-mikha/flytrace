Click it to manually update the page. Note that there is also an automatic update that happens every 30 sec. Pressing this button is faster than refreshing the whole page in a browser (e.g. by pressing F5).
Shows when the page was updated. It happens either automatically every 30 seconds, or after pressing the "Update Now" link.
Inside is the first letter of the marker name. Here the name is James, so the letter is 'J'. For Anna it would be 'A' and so on. If there are James and Jason, there would be two J markers.

You could click markers to see their details (coordinates, link to show Full Track etc))
Shows that 5 minutes ago the tracker has sent a signal from the point where you see the marker now. It is time when the position was SENT, not when it was received. So it means that James has been there 5 minutes ago from NOW.

On a real map (this one is just a screenshot) the value would update with a time passing, and after a minute it will automatically change to '6 min'.
The green line is the latest segment of track: it goes from the previous point to the latest position. Note that trackers send coordinates every 10 minutes. So this line gives you an instant idea whether tracker moves or not.

For example, if this line is 10 kms long then probably the tracker still moves. But when it's just 10 meters long (and can be seen only at a highest zoom) it means that most likely the tracker has stopped.
